PART 6 - DEMO SCENARIO (INTERVIEW MICROSERVICE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Fluid, clear and structured scenario to guide the demonstration and highlight advanced features.

----------------------------------------------------------------
0. PRE-REQUISITES
----------------------------------------------------------------
0.1 Backend and frontend are running, seed data loaded (projects, candidatures, interviews, availability slots, notifications).
0.2 You know the test accounts you will use, for example:
    - Client user (project owner)
    - Freelancer A
    - Freelancer B
    - Admin (back-office)

Try to always keep the same three personas during the demo:
    - "Client Claire" (owner of one or more projects)
    - "Freelancer Farid"
    - "Admin Alice"

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. ACT 1 - FREELANCER SETS AVAILABILITY (FEEDS ADVANCED FEATURES)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: Show how availability slots feed interview creation, workload, and suggestions.

1.1 Log in as Freelancer Farid.
1.2 Navigate to "Mes disponibilités" (freelancer availability page).
1.3 Show existing seeded slots on the calendar/list (if any).
1.4 Create a batch of new slots:
    - Select date range: next 7-14 days
    - Time window: for example 09:00-12:00
    - Slot duration: e.g. 30 minutes
    - Click "Générer / Enregistrer"
1.5 Explain:
    - These slots will be used by clients as preferred times.
    - The system uses them for:
        - Alternative slot suggestions (when conflicts happen)
        - Workload computation (busy vs light)

Do not go deep into interviews yet; just plant the idea that availability is the base.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. ACT 2 - CLIENT CREATES INTERVIEW AND TRIGGERS SLOT SUGGESTIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: Show full "create" flow and the advanced alternative suggestions.

2.1 Log in as Client Claire.
2.2 Go to "Mes projets", open a project that already has candidatures (seeded).
2.3 Open the "Entretiens" / interview tab for that project.

Scenario A - Happy path:
2.4 Pick Freelancer Farid from the candidate list.
2.5 Choose a date/time that matches one of Farid's availability slots (e.g. tomorrow at 10:00, 30 min).
2.6 Choose mode ONLINE or FACE_TO_FACE.
2.7 Click "Créer l'entretien".
2.8 Show result:
    - New interview row appears in the project's interview list.
    - Status = PROPOSED.
    - For ONLINE: there is a future visio slot (but not yet joinable).

Scenario B - Trigger alternative suggestions:
2.9 Try to schedule another interview for the same freelancer, but this time:
    - Use a time that conflicts with an existing confirmed interview, or
    - Use a time outside the freelancer's availability window.
2.10 Click "Créer l'entretien".
2.11 Show:
    - Error message explaining the conflict.
    - The "suggestions" area listing alternative slots (from Farid's availability and conflict resolution algorithm).
2.12 Click one suggestion:
    - The form is auto-filled with suggested date/time and mode.
    - Click "Créer l'entretien" again.
2.13 Highlight: 
    - The system automatically searches the next 14 days, filters by availability and existing interviews, and proposes the best alternatives.
    - Great for UX: Client is guided instead of manually trying random times.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. ACT 3 - PER-ROW RELIABILITY (FIABILITÉ) IN THE CLIENT VIEW
----------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: Show that each freelancer has his own reliability visible per interview row.

3.1 Still as Client Claire, go to the global "Mes entretiens" page (client role).
3.2 Explain the columns:
    - Date / time
    - Time left
    - "Fiabilité freelancer" column (new per-row column)
    - Mode, Status, Actions

3.3 Point to two or three rows with different freelancers:
    - For each row, the "Fiabilité freelancer" shows that specific freelancer's reliability score.
    - This is not one global score; it is per freelancer based on his history (COMPLETED, NO_SHOW, CANCELLED).
3.4 Explain quickly how it is computed:
    - Completed interviews increase score.
    - No-shows and cancellations lower it.
    - Time window of past interviews is taken into account.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. ACT 4 - FREELANCER VIEW: WORKLOAD AND CLIENT RELIABILITY
----------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: Each client has his own reliability score from the freelancer's point of view, and workload is shown.

4.1 Log in as Freelancer Farid again.
4.2 Go to "Mes entretiens" (freelancer view).
4.3 Show the stats strip:
    - "Charge : …" (workload label)
        - Example: "normal (3 interviews, 2.0h next 7 days)"
    - Explain: this classifies workload based on total minutes and number of interviews in the next 7 days.

4.4 Show table columns:
    - "Fiabilité client" column (per row)
4.5 Highlight:
    - For each interview row, the fiabilité shows the reliability of that specific client (ownerId) towards freelancers.
    - This helps freelancers evaluate if a client tends to cancel late or not show up.

4.6 If possible, show different scores for different clients (use seeded data or pre-run some no-show/cancel scenarios beforehand).

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. ACT 5 - LIFECYCLE: CONFIRM, CANCEL (WITH RULE), COMPLETE, NO-SHOW
----------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: Show the advanced business rules on top of basic CRUD.

5.1 Use an interview in the future that is currently PROPOSED (created in Act 2 or from seed).
5.2 As Freelancer Farid in "Mes entretiens":
    - Click "Accepter" on a PROPOSED interview.
    - Status becomes CONFIRMED; PROPOSED actions disappear.

5.3 Show cancellation rule:
    - As Client Claire, go to "Mes entretiens".
    - Find the CONFIRMED interview with start time less than 24 hours away (seeded or you wait).
    - Try to "Annuler".
    - System should refuse cancellation if under configured threshold (e.g. 24h) and suggest marking NO_SHOW later.
    - Show the error message.

5.4 Show valid cancellation:
    - Use another CONFIRMED interview that is more than 24h in the future.
    - As Client or Admin, click "Annuler".
    - Status becomes CANCELLED.
    - Explain that availability slot is released so someone else can be scheduled.

5.5 Show completion:
    - Use a CONFIRMED interview that is in the near past or you simulate "after the meeting".
    - As Freelancer, click "Terminé".
    - Status becomes COMPLETED.
    - Mention that only COMPLETED interviews are eligible for reviews and impact reliability.

5.6 Show no-show:
    - Use a CONFIRMED interview whose end time is already in the past (seeded).
    - As Client or Freelancer, the "No show" button appears in the actions.
    - Click "No show".
    - Status becomes NO_SHOW.
    - Explain how this penalizes reliability of the user who did not show up.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. ACT 6 - VISIO ROOM WITH ACCESS WINDOW
----------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: Show the in-app visio integration with strict time window.

6.1 Use an ONLINE, CONFIRMED interview starting "now" (one of the seeded ones or scheduled earlier).
6.2 As Client Claire:
    - In "Mes entretiens", find that row.
    - The "Rejoindre" link should be active only between startAt and endAt.
6.3 Click "Rejoindre".
    - You are taken to /client/interviews/{id}/visio.
    - The page fetches interview details, validates time window, requests or creates a visio room, and embeds it (e.g. Jitsi).
6.4 Show that if you open the same URL before or after the allowed window:
    - The link is disabled or
    - The page shows an error / "Indisponible".

6.5 Optionally, repeat as Freelancer Farid:
    - Show the symmetric route /freelancer/interviews/{id}/visio.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7. ACT 7 - ADMIN BACK-OFFICE: GLOBAL VIEW AND FILTERING
----------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: Show that the microservice supports back-office needs: global search, filters, pagination.

7.1 Log in as Admin Alice.
7.2 Go to "Entretiens" in the admin sidebar (global list).
7.3 Show:
    - Filters: status, mode, projectId, freelancerId, ownerId, from, to.
    - Pagination controls, 5 rows per page.
7.4 Demo a few use cases:
    - Filter all CONFIRMED ONLINE interviews this week.
    - Filter by a specific freelancerId to see their schedule and reliability context.
    - Filter by projectId to see all interviews attached to a specific mission.
7.5 Explain:
    - This is all served by the same Interview microservice search endpoint with pagination and sorting.

9. CLOSING SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------
End the demo by summarizing what the microservice covers, all visible only via UI:

    - CRUD on interviews with strong business rules (one interview per pair, cancellation windows, etc.)
    - Advanced features:
        - Availability management and batch slot creation
        - Smart alternative suggestions for time conflicts
        - Per-person reliability:
            - Client sees each freelancer's reliability per row
            - Freelancer sees each client's reliability per row
        - Workload classification for freelancers
        - Time-window-controlled visio rooms
        - Back-office search with filtering and pagination

This scenario should flow naturally from availability to scheduling to lifecycle to visio to analytics (reliability and workload) to admin control, while visually surfacing all advanced features.
